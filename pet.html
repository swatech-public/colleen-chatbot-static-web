Pets


Southwest Airlines accepts small, vaccinated, domestic cats and dogs to travel as pets. All in-cabin pets must be carried in an appropriate carrier. A pet carrier counts as a personal item or a carryon item and is subject to a one-way fare of $95.00 per pet carrier. (Note: Pets are not allowed to travel from the U.S. mainland to Hawaii or from Hawaii to the U.S. mainland, but are permitted to travel between the Hawaiian Islands.) Southwest Airlines allows only one pet carrier per ticketed Customer. The carrier may contain two cats or dogs and must be of the same species per carrier. The cat or dog must be able to stand up and move around the carrier with ease. A Customer traveling with a cat or dog must check in with the pet at the airport ticket counter and pay the Pet Fare before going to the departure gate. Customers can secure their boarding passes online, at curbside checkin, a kiosk, or the ticket counter.

Southwest Airlines maintains the right to refuse acceptance of a cat or dog exhibiting aggressive behavior or any characteristics that appear incompatible with air travel. Customers traveling with a pet may not occupy an exit seat or a bulkhead seat. Southwest Airlines only carries pets in the cabin; pet carriers containing animals are not permitted as checked baggage. Pets must be at least eight weeks old to travel. The cat or dog must be harmless, not disruptive, odorless, and require no attention during flight. Pets must remain in the carrier (including head and tail) while in the gate area, during boarding, the entire duration of the flight, and during deplaning. Southwest Airlines assumes no liability for the health or well-being of carryon pets.

Customers who pay a Pet Fare and are traveling with their pet may check a pet crate or stroller in addition to the free baggage allowance without charge.


Service Animals


Under the Air Carrier Access Act, a trained service animal can be individually trained or licensed/certified by a state or local government or affiliated with a service animal training school. Note: A service animal vest, harness, ID card, or registration is not accepted as the sole indication an animal is a trained service animal. Southwest Airlines maintains the right to refuse acceptance of a Service Animal exhibiting aggressive behavior or any characteristics that appear incompatible with air travel. The U.S. Department of Transportation provides the needed Service Animal Air Transportation Form which can be found here. The form can be printed or completed online and printed at home. Please being the completed form with you to the airport for your travel.
